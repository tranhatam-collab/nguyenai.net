# Nguyễn AI / Nguyen AI — AI Safety Policy

## Core rule

Nguyen AI is a sourced heritage intelligence system. AI must support research, not invent history.

## Every research answer must display

- sources used;
- relevant excerpts;
- source date;
- source type;
- confidence level;
- uncertainty points;
- variants or disputes;
- warning when evidence is insufficient.

## Required AI behavior

The AI must be able to answer:

> Chưa có đủ nguồn đáng tin cậy để kết luận.

> There is not enough reliable source evidence to conclude.

## Forbidden AI behavior

AI must not:

- create ancestor names;
- connect two branches only because names are similar;
- confirm royal lineage;
- confirm bloodline;
- publish private living-person information;
- mistranslate personal names as ordinary words;
- replace Hán-Nôm specialists;
- present inference as fact;
- upgrade weak evidence into verified status.

## Claim confidence rules

- A: primary source or multiple independent checkable sources.
- B: trustworthy source but incomplete continuity.
- C: branch genealogy or secondary source not fully cross-checked.
- D: oral history, inference or unverified content.

AI can propose a confidence level but human/editorial workflow must control final verified labels.

## Human-in-the-loop requirements

Human review is required for:

- public historical claims;
- profile verification;
- founder verified badges;
- disputed lineage claims;
- royal lineage references;
- publication of sensitive archival documents;
- corrections requested by living people or families.

## Safety evaluation before launch

Test prompts must include attempts to make the AI:

- invent missing ancestors;
- claim Nguyễn Phúc lineage without evidence;
- expose living-person private data;
- merge branches based only on surname;
- overstate confidence;
- ignore contradictory sources;
- output unsupported SEO content.

Launch is blocked if the assistant cannot refuse or qualify unsupported claims.
